# Tagline Candidates for IPA Sound Drill

This document collects tagline candidates for IPA Sound Drill, with voice/tone evaluation, emotional profile, and 6-language translation considerations. Candidates are for testing in Claude Design prototypes and final selection at Phase 3 (launch materials).

## Voice & Tone Constraints

All taglines must align with:
- **Product Voice**: "Quiet mentor" (静かな師範) — deep knowledge without display, respect for learner autonomy
- **Prohibited vocabulary**: "master", "perfect", "native", "unlock", "AI-powered" (see Voice-and-Tone Taboo List)
- **Emotional register**: Restrained, not excited. Aesthetic, not utilitarian.

## English Candidates

### E-1: "Drill your English sounds with IPA."

- **Voice fit**: ✅ "Drill" evokes artisan repetition; "your" emphasizes learner ownership
- **Emotional profile**: Direct, actionable, quietly confident
- **Differentiation from competitors**:
  - Duolingo would say "Learn English"
  - ELSA Speak would say "Master pronunciation with AI"
  - IPA Sound Drill: "Drill" + "IPA" = artisan + tool
- **P-1 resonance**: Medium — practical but doesn't touch "deep conversation" motivation
- **6-language translatability**: Moderate. Japanese: 「IPA で英語の音をドリルする」(natural). Korean/Chinese: "drill" concept may need adaptation.

### E-2: "The IPA-driven pronunciation drill."

- **Voice fit**: ✅ Descriptive, calm, article-first (The...)
- **Emotional profile**: Neutral, positioning-focused
- **Differentiation**: Positions the product as a category (IPA-driven drill) vs feature-based competitors
- **P-1 resonance**: Low — too descriptive, no emotional pull
- **6-language translatability**: High. Works in any language as a category description.

### E-3: "Retune your English. From sound up."

- **Voice fit**: ✅ "Retune" is quiet + artisan. "From sound up" emphasizes IPA foundation
- **Emotional profile**: Restrained ambition, promising rebuild
- **Differentiation**: Suggests the current state needs adjustment, but respectfully (not "your accent is broken")
- **P-1 resonance**: High — matches "音を練り直す" and P-1's Japanized-English self-awareness
- **6-language translatability**: Medium. "Retune" has no direct equivalent in some languages; Japanese: 「英語を、音から練り直す」

### E-4: "For deep conversations, in English."

- **Voice fit**: ✅ Direct address to the goal, quiet
- **Emotional profile**: Aspirational but grounded
- **Differentiation**: Motivation-focused (not skill-focused). Rare in the category.
- **P-1 resonance**: Very high — this is exactly P-1's motivation
- **6-language translatability**: High. Japanese: 「深い話がしたい人に、英語を」. Korean/Chinese: works well.

### E-5: "Sound before word."

- **Voice fit**: ✅ Aphoristic, short
- **Emotional profile**: Provocative in a quiet way (SLA-influenced learners will recognize this)
- **Differentiation**: Emphasizes Mode B (sound-first vocabulary) — unique positioning
- **P-1 resonance**: Medium — resonates for those who understand the pedagogy
- **6-language translatability**: Very high. Concise, universal.

## Japanese Candidates

### J-1: 「IPA で、英語の音をドリルする。」

- Voice fit: ✅ Reference to en E-1, natural Japanese
- Emotional profile: Craftsperson-like, quietly confident
- P-1 resonance: Medium
- Usage: Body copy, subtitle

### J-2: 「音でドリル、IPA で解剖。」

- Voice fit: ✅ Parallel structure, "解剖" evokes academic depth
- Emotional profile: Intellectual, precision-oriented
- P-1 resonance: High (P-1 values intellectual engagement)
- Usage: LP hero, marketing

### J-3: 「深い話がしたい人に、英語を。」

- Voice fit: ✅ Direct address, quiet, respectful
- Emotional profile: Aspirational, mature, connective
- P-1 resonance: Very high (direct echo of P-1's stated motivation)
- Usage: LP hero
- **Note**: Best P-1 fit among all candidates

### J-4: 「英語を、音から練り直す。」

- Voice fit: ✅ "練り直す" is craftsperson-like
- Emotional profile: Rebuilding, honest about starting from a Japanized baseline
- P-1 resonance: High (matches P-1's self-awareness)
- Usage: LP subtitle, body

### J-5: 「音を、美しく。」

- Voice fit: ✅ Aesthetic, restrained
- Emotional profile: Emphasizes P-1's "beautiful pronunciation" success metric
- P-1 resonance: High (matches "発音が綺麗だね" success moment)
- Usage: LP hero (alternative to J-3), Track B (music-driven, P-5) aligned

## 6-Language Localization Notes

For final tagline selection, consider these translation considerations:

| Language | Notes |
|---|---|
| **English (en)** | Primary. Set the master tone. |
| **Japanese (ja)** | 丁寧語 base, avoid casualness (「〜だよ」) and over-formality (「〜でございます」). |
| **Korean (ko)** | 합니다체 (formal but not stiff). Avoid 하십시오체 (too formal) and 하다체 (informal). Consider Sino-Korean vs native Korean vocabulary balance. |
| **Chinese Simplified (zh-Hans)** | Prefer concise 4-6 character phrases. Avoid Taiwan-specific vocabulary. Consider whether "IPA" stays as-is or is called 国际音标. |
| **Chinese Traditional (zh-Hant)** | Similar to zh-Hans but with Traditional characters. "國際音標" for IPA. |
| **Filipino (fil)** | Avoid overly casual "ka" or overly formal "po/ho". Natural, respectful register. IPA generally stays as English acronym. |

## Testing Strategy in Phase 1

1. **Cluster 1 (Top page)**: Test **J-3** (main) and **J-5** (alternative) as hero taglines
2. **Cluster 2 (Visual language)**: Test **J-2** (intellectual) vs **J-5** (aesthetic) as brand tone anchors
3. **English LP**: Test **E-3** (rebuild) vs **E-4** (deep conversations)

## Final Selection Criteria

For Phase 3 (launch materials) final selection:

1. **P-1 resonance**: Since P-1 is the primary Track A persona, high resonance is required
2. **6-language translatability**: Must work in all 6 languages without loss of voice
3. **Differentiation from competitors**: Should not read as "another Duolingo" or "another ELSA"
4. **Voice & Tone alignment**: Zero taboo vocabulary, quiet mentor voice

## Prohibited Directions (What We Don't Do)

Following the Anti-patterns Catalog (see private working documents), taglines will NOT:

- Use "master", "perfect", "native", "unlock", "AI-powered"
- Promise unrealistic outcomes ("Fluent in 30 days")
- Use gamification vocabulary ("Level up", "Streak")
- Personify the product ("I'll help you...")
- Emphasize speed or urgency ("Fast", "Now", "Today")
